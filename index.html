What are the costs of driving?

From 2002 to 2022, petrol prices in Australia rose steadily due to global and domestic factors. Initially low, prices increased as global demand for oil grew and supply tightened. Although the 2008 global financial crisis caused temporary dips, prices rebounded as economies recovered. By the 2010s, geopolitical tensions and currency fluctuations further drove prices up. While the COVID-19 pandemic briefly decreased demand, supply chain disruptions and inflation in 2022 led to a sharp surge in petrol prices, affecting households and industries across the country.
